Frequently Asked Questions

The longevity of your roof depends on various factors such as the materials, installation, and ventilation. Typically a strong, well-constructed roof will last between 12-15 years. Depending on the quality, the type of shingles you choose, and the underlayment of your roof the number can go up to 30 and more.

Summer and fall are usually busier with roof-replacing services. Factors like snow, rain, and ice can impact how quickly the roof contractors can effectively and safely get your roof finished that’s why drier months are preferred for roof repair services.

The main sign is the frequency of a specific problem. For example, if your roof has been leaking and even after the repair process the problem continues then you definitely need a roof replacement process.

The market is full of roofing materials. You can choose whatever color and shape you want. To make the right choice you need to know how you want your roof to look. When choosing the right material you should also consider the type of material, weather conditions, and price.

The roof replacement process creates a lot of mess and waste. In case your old roofing material is in a bad condition then you can recycle it. Otherwise, if it is in a good condition you can choose to donate it.

When repairs have been made but have failed to address your problems, replacing your roof is advised. Also, take into account the age of your roof; if it is more than 50 or 60 years old, a replacement is ultimately a better choice.
